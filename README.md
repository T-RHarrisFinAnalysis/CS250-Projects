# CS-250: Software Development Lifecycle

## Course Description

This repository contains coursework, assignments, journals, and projects completed for **CS-250: Software Development Lifecycle** at Southern New Hampshire University (SNHU).

The course explored the Software Development Lifecycle (SDLC), Agile development methodologies, Scrum practices, software documentation, testing, project planning, and collaboration throughout the software development process.

Reflection Journal

How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating “user stories” help with this?
Throughout this course, I learned the importance of understanding user needs and translating them into clear user stories. User stories helped the team focus on delivering value in small, manageable pieces while staying aligned with stakeholder priorities.

How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?
My approach to software development has embraced Agile principles such as iterative planning, continuous feedback, and adaptive change. I hope to carry forward practices like sprint planning, daily stand-ups, and retrospectives into future projects to foster collaboration and improve quality.

What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?
Being a good team member means communicating openly, supporting others, and being accountable. Agile taught me that team success depends on shared responsibility and trust.

Applying Scrum and Agile methods gave me hands-on experience with flexible, customer-focused development. This will be invaluable as I take on more complex projects in my career.
